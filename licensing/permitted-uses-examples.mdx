**You can build and sell commercial products using HDK.**

This page enumerates concrete scenarios with explicit permitted / restricted
labels. For the operative definitions and the reasoning behind the
license choice, see [the FAQ](./faq).

## Permitted ✅

### Shipping commercial products built on HDK

- A paid intelligent inbox app sold on the Mac App Store
- An "Excel-with-AI" desktop app sold to enterprises with per-seat pricing
- A medical diagnostic device with HDK embedded for local inference, sold
  commercially to hospitals
- A car infotainment system shipped by an OEM with HDK powering the
  in-car AI assistant
- A consumer mobile app with on-device intelligent features (HDK via
  `react-native-lloyal` once shipped)
- A B2B SaaS that uses HDK on the customer's servers to power its agent
  workflows

### Vertical AI products built on HDK

- "Clay for legal" — a sales-intelligence app for law firms, built on HDK
- "Cowork for healthcare" — a workspace-AI app for clinicians, built on HDK
- A workflow-automation product that lets users describe pipelines and
  watches HDK agents execute them
- An intelligent IDE extension that uses HDK for local code understanding

The framing test: are you building *with* HDK, or are you offering HDK
itself? Building with — permitted. Offering HDK — restricted.

### Professional services

- A consulting firm builds a custom intelligent harness for a Fortune 500
  client, charges $500K for the engagement, and deploys it on the client's
  infrastructure
- An agency wraps HDK into a per-vertical solution and sells implementation
  services
- A solo dev offers HDK integration as a freelance service

### Internal enterprise use

- A bank runs HDK internally to power their intelligent search and document
  understanding for compliance use
- A government agency deploys HDK on-premise for sovereign AI use cases
- A research org uses HDK across hundreds of internal applications

### Research and education

- A university lab uses HDK in published academic research on agent
  architectures
- A computer science course teaches HDK as part of an "AI systems" curriculum
- A non-commercial open-source project builds on HDK and re-shares its
  contributions

### Modification and redistribution

- Forking HDK on GitHub to learn, prototype, or contribute back
- Modifying HDK internals for an internal performance experiment
- Redistributing HDK (with the same FSL terms) as part of an open-source
  bundle or internal package mirror
- Building an HDK App and publishing it to `apps.lloyal.ai` (the canonical channel — every listed App is reviewed by Lloyal Labs for tool-safety and manifest conformance, and consumers get a single AI-safety boundary across every HDK harness)

## Restricted ❌

### Becoming the replacement HDK platform

- AWS / Google / Microsoft launching "Bedrock Managed HDK" or "Vertex HDK"
  or "Azure HDK" as a hosted runtime service
- A competitor publishing "OpenHDK" as a forked harness runtime under a
  different name, offered as a substitute
- A clean-room reimplementation of HDK in Python, Rust, or Go intended as a
  drop-in replacement runtime
- A vendor offering "Managed HDK Hosting" or "HDK-as-a-Service" as a
  competing SaaS

### Operating an alternative App distribution channel

The restriction here is consumer-protective: fragmenting App distribution
across uncurated channels would split the AI-safety review surface, leave
consumers reasoning about multiple trust boundaries, and let the same
"App" mean different things on different harnesses. Examples of what's
restricted:

- Launching `apps.competitor.com` (or any similar service) as an alternative
  HDK App distribution channel
- Building a "compatible App marketplace" that lets HDK developers publish
  outside of `apps.lloyal.ai`

### Offering HDK-equivalent services

- A hosted orchestration service that exposes HDK-compatible APIs as a
  substitute for the HDK runtime
- A managed-inference product that wraps HDK and resells it as your own
  inference platform

## Borderline / ask if unsure

- Building a product that *includes* an App distribution channel for users
  to install plugins, where those plugins are not strictly HDK Apps — likely
  permitted, but the line depends on how strongly the plugin model
  resembles HDK's App protocol
- Offering HDK consulting at scale with a managed-deployment offering —
  permitted if the deployment is on the customer's infrastructure;
  borderline if you're providing the infrastructure as well
- A platform that allows third parties to deploy "agent runtimes" generally,
  where HDK is one option among many — depends on the platform's positioning

For borderline cases, email [legal@lloyal.ai](mailto:legal@lloyal.ai). The goal is to give you a
quick yes and clarify intent; we want the products you're building to ship.

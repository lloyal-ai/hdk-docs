## Who this applies to

If you've run [`harness.dev publishers register`](/cli/publishing) and
claimed a handle, you are a **publisher** under these terms. Each
subsequent submission is made under the handle you registered.

You retain ownership of the code in your app. These terms govern only
the distribution relationship: what you grant Lloyal Labs to do with
your tarball, what Lloyal Labs commits to do in return, and the rules
your submissions must meet.

## What you grant Lloyal Labs

By submitting a tarball through `harness.dev publish`, you grant Lloyal
Labs a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to:

1. **Inspect the tarball** during review — including extracting and
   reading `package.json`, the bundled source, the manifest, and the
   `app.json` metadata.
2. **Sign the approved tarball bytes** with the Lloyal platform Ed25519
   key, producing a manifest carrying that signature.
3. **Distribute the signed tarball + manifest + catalog entry** via the
   `apps.lloyal.ai` channel and any successor channel under the same
   trust roots.
4. **Cache and serve** the signed tarball through Cloudflare R2 and any
   CDN layer in front of it.
5. **Display your handle and the catalog name** publicly in the catalog
   and in any portal Lloyal Labs operates over the channel.

The signature is over the exact bytes you submitted; Lloyal Labs does
not modify your tarball before signing. If a revision is needed, you
submit a new version under a new semver.

## What Lloyal Labs commits to

1. **Review every submission** before it enters the catalog.
2. **Communicate review outcomes** — approval includes the canonical
   tarball URL; rejection includes a written reason.
3. **Preserve rejected submission artifacts for 30 days** so you can
   appeal or resubmit a corrected version.
4. **Not modify your tarball bytes.** Approved bytes are exactly the
   submitted bytes.
5. **Maintain the catalog signature** so consumers verifying against
   `CHANNEL_TRUST_ROOTS` can rely on its integrity.
6. **Honor reasonable rate limits** (currently 10 publishes per 24h per
   publisher identity).

You agree that your submissions:

1. **Will not contain malicious code.** This includes (non-exhaustively):

2. **Will not scrape or transmit personal data** of harness end-users
   without their explicit informed consent.
3. **Will not impersonate** another publisher, person, or organization.
4. **Will respect third-party rights.** You are responsible for ensuring

5. **Will conform to the [HDK App Protocol](/build-an-app/what-is-an-app)**

6. **Will use a `package.json` name that you control** and that matches
   the `importName` field declared in your manifest stub.

## Rejection reasons

Lloyal Labs may reject a submission for any of:

- Failure to meet any obligation in the previous section.
- Tool surface overlap with existing approved apps that creates
  significant consumer confusion.
- Manifest claims that don't match the tarball contents.
- Insufficient documentation for a reviewer to assess the app's behavior.

A rejection is per-submission; you may resubmit a corrected tarball
under the same or a new version.

## Revocation

Lloyal Labs may revoke a published app from the catalog for:

1. **A subsequent finding** that the app violates these terms.
2. **A credible security report** about the app's behavior.
3. **A legal order** requiring removal.
4. **A request from the publisher** to delist a version.

Revocation removes the catalog entry and the canonical R2 paths.
Consumers who previously installed the app retain their local copy —
the tarball bytes on their disk are not modified.

## Suspension

Lloyal Labs may suspend a publisher account — preventing further
submissions, without removing previously approved apps — for:

1. Repeated submission of apps that violate these terms.
2. Abuse of the review pipeline (e.g., spam submissions to exhaust
   reviewer capacity).
3. Behavior that misrepresents Lloyal Labs or another publisher.

Suspended publishers may appeal by emailing
[`support@lloyal.ai`](mailto:support@lloyal.ai).

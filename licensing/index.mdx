**You can build and sell commercial products using HDK.**

> HDK is free to build products with; it is not free to become the
> replacement HDK platform.

HDK 3.0 runtime packages are source-available under **FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0**
(a [Fair Source](https://fair.io) license) and convert to Apache 2.0 two
years after each release. The restriction during those two years is narrow:
you cannot offer a competing HDK runtime, managed HDK service, or alternative
HDK App distribution channel. The canonical App distribution channel is
`apps.lloyal.ai` — every App listed there is reviewed by Lloyal Labs for
tool-safety and manifest conformance before publication, so consumers can
rely on a single AI-safety boundary across every HDK harness.

The `harness.dev` CLI and `hdk-create-app` scaffolder are Apache 2.0 —
unrestricted.

## License-by-package summary

| Component | License |
|---|---|
| `liblloyal`, `lloyal-node` | FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0 |
| `@lloyal-labs/lloyal-agents`, `@lloyal-labs/lloyal-sdk`, `@lloyal-labs/rig` | FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0 |
| `@lloyal-labs/corpus`, `@lloyal-labs/web` | FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0 |
| `@lloyal-labs/harness-cli` (the `harness.dev` CLI) | **Apache 2.0** |
| `hdk-create-app` (when shipped) | **Apache 2.0** |
| `llama.cpp` (vendored dependency) | MIT (unchanged) |

Each component's repo carries an in-tree copy of the licensing FAQ next to
the LICENSE file (`LICENSE-FAQ.md`) so the explanation is at the point of
contact. This page is the canonical version; the in-repo copies are
synchronized to it.

## Questions?

If you have a use case and aren't sure whether it falls under Permitted
Purpose or Competing Use, email <legal@lloyal.ai> or open a discussion in
the repo. We'd rather give you a quick yes than have you worry about it.
